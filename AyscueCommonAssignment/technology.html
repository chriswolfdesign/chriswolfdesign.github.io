Technology in the Classroom

Technology has offered educators a wide variety of new tools to use in their classrooms. They allow us to communicate with students in ways that fit their 21st century lifestyle and can reinforce techniques they will use outside of the classroom. Below, I will discuss three technologies I plan to use in the classroom to aid my students learning.

DISCLAIMER: While technology can greatly assist educators it is important to note that technology cannot and will not replace researched and confirmed best pedagogical practices.

Technology I: SmartMusic

SmartMusic is an online program that allows students to receive immediate feedback on their performance when their director is not available. SmartMusic also allows the educator to set playing tests to be given an infinite number of trails, allowing students to take the test as many times as needed to demonstrate mastery of the piece's concepts. This permits directors to not only access mastery but also assess the equally important concept of growth. A more recent accomplishment of SmartMusic is their adjustment towards a browser-based client. With the recent interest school districts have had in Chromebooks, this allows these schools to continue using SmartMusic. This also allows students to access SmartMusic from their own computers at home.

Technology II: PowerPoint

PowerPoint is a cliche tool for educators but for good reason. PowerPoints can be used to display information in a concise way for students to have easy access. However, the overuse of this technology can cause the learning experience to become stagnant and lose student interest. I have found a successful balance to be using PowerPoint to display the rehearsal schedule on the board. This allows students to prepare their materials for rehearsal in an organized way but shifts the focus to music making once rehearsal starts.

Technology III: Tonal Energy

Tonal Energy is a mobile app that students can use to tune their instruments, assess their rhythmic accuracy, and even record themselves to listen and judge their own musical phrasing and shaping. While Tonal Energy Pro does cost a marginal amount, they do offer a free version with advertisements. Tonal Energy is current available for Android and iOS devices.
