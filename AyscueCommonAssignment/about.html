About Me as an Educator

My Reason

I teach for the feeling I get when a student finally understands what we have been working on in class. I teach to help spread music to the world. I teach to share my love of music with children. I teach to give every child I teach an opportunity to become the best versions of themselves they can be. The most important reason that I teach, I learned from a renowned band director, Scott Rush. Mr. Rush said "The truth is, what we do is about making music and using music to affect young lives." 1 So the most important reason I teach is to make music and share that experience with my students.

Purpose of Education

The purpose of education is to give knowledge to children and help shape them into successful and productive members of society. It is important in the world today because without education we will have ignorance in the world. As long as we live, we need to strive to give children an education to make them strong members of society and good people. We should not strive for good test scores; that is not the value of education. Education should not be based on numbers because intelligence is not based on statistics. Education must be derived from the drive and willingness children have to learn because once we instill those values into them, education finally becomes about the individual, how the individual learns, and how they can pursue life-long learning.

Who I am

I see myself as the leader of my classroom. This is necessary when I have 3+ young musicians looking to me for guidance to achieving success on their instrument. In my school, I am a leader because I can inspire at least one person a day to be their best through musical excellence. In my community, I am a leader because I work to acquire better funding and support for the arts so all students can experience the beauty of making music.

My Approach

I as an educator will have a growth mindset in the classroom. To accomplish this, I will use "The Seven Habits of Highly Effective People" by Stephen R. Covey2 to help shape my educational approach. The Habits are a great way to structure your classroom because these are methods I have seen achieve success.

Grade Level Goals
Upon completing sixth grade, band students will be able to:
  • Produce a fundamental tone on their instrument
  • Perform at least five major scales in one octave
  • Map fundamental rhythms to Edward Lisk's Ruler of Time3
  • Perform Grade 1-2 music on the North Carolina Standard Concert Band Repertoire List4

Upon completing seventh grade, band students will be able to:
  • Demonstrate a developing tone on their instrument
  • Perform at least ten major scales in one octave
  • Begin demonstrating a basic understanding of musical phrasing
  • Map moderate rhythms to Edward Lisk's Ruler of Time
  • Perform Grade 2-3 music on the North Carolina Standard Concert Band Repertoire List

Upon completing eighth grade, band students will be able to:
  • Achieve success in high school music ensembles
  • Demonstrate a mature tone on their instrument
  • Perform all twelve major scales in two octaves
  • Make mature musical decisions in terms of shape and phrasing
  • Map advanced rhythms to Edward Lisk's Ruler of Time
  • Perform Grade 3-4 music on the North Carolina Standard Concert Band Repertoire List
