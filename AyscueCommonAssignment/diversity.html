Diversity in the Classroom

Since taking EDCI, I have learned that there are many things about diversity that I have not yet learned and I should continue studying so when I teach, I can ensure I am doing my best as an educator to include all students in the classroom culture. Diversity is an important concept to understand as a teacher because it helps me ensure students feel safe, welcome, and appreciated in my classroom. Without attention to diversity, educators can unintentionally be insensitive to students' opinions and needs. Through this course, I have learned to focus on learning about a variety of cultures and ethnic backgrounds to create a more cohesive and progressive educational experience for my students.

As an educator with a new awareness of diversity, I have learned to be more aware of what my students are experiencing, inside and outside of my classroom. An important goal for any teacher is to encourage students to feel comfortable approaching us with their own thoughts and opinions, including: culture, ethnicity, sexual orientation, and race. These topics, while sensitive, are a large part of our students lives and it is our responsibility as educators to help them navigate through these difficulties. All students must be given the same opportunities in the classroom regardless of traits that may make them different from others. These differences must be celebrated, not rejected.

As a music educator, I must include diversity into my curriculum. The most obvious way to accomplish this is through performance of pieces derived from a variety of cultures and backgrounds. Each year, I will have students choose a piece from our repertoire and discuss the culture the piece was inspired by and what they believe the composer was attempting to accomplish in their composition. Music is a wonderful vehicle to explore the world because music transcends human language. All cultures have their own musical history and I am obligated as a music educator to share this beauty with my students.
